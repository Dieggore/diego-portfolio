About
01

The engineer who couldn't stop building.

I wrote my first line of code back in middle school — just messing around, figuring things out. But it was at UACH in Chihuahua where I started building software in a way that actually made sense. Something about the idea that you could build an entire world from a blank screen felt unreasonably powerful.

That feeling never went away.

Professionally, I've spent the last 15 years building products at companies that push the edge: Nearsoft, Siemens Healthineers, Xapo Bank, and Aircore — Sean Parker's social platform.

But the work I'm most proud of isn't in anyone else's codebase. It's in the pharmacies across Chihuahua that stopped getting compliance fines because of Livora. In the country clubs that replaced their Excel sheets and phone calls with El Marshal.

I care deeply about code quality, performance, and UI/UX — not as buzzwords, but because I've seen what happens when you don't. I'm a craftsperson. I build things I'd want to use myself.

One more thing: I have a Master's degree in Gestalt Psychotherapy. I bring that into how I think about users, teams, and products. Software is ultimately made for humans — and understanding humans is half the job.
